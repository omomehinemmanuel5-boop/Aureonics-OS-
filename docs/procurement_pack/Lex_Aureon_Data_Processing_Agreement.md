# Lex Aureon Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Effective Date]  
**Between:** [Customer Legal Entity] (“Controller” or “Customer”) and [Lex Aureon Contracting Entity] (“Processor”)

## 1. Purpose and Scope
This DPA governs Processing of Personal Data by Processor on behalf of Customer in connection with the Lex Aureon AI Output Governance Control Plane services (“Services”).

## 2. Definitions
- **Applicable Data Protection Law:** all privacy and data protection laws applicable to Processing under this DPA, including GDPR where applicable.
- **Personal Data, Processing, Controller, Processor, Data Subject, Supervisory Authority:** have meanings given under Applicable Data Protection Law.
- **Security Incident:** confirmed accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data Processed by Processor.

## 3. Roles of the Parties
Customer acts as Controller (or Processor with authority from Controller) and Processor acts as Processor (or Subprocessor where Customer is Processor), as defined by Applicable Data Protection Law.

## 4. Processing Details
### 4.1 Subject Matter
Provision of AI output governance, analysis, policy enforcement, and audit logging services.

### 4.2 Duration
For the term of the underlying services agreement, plus deletion/return period described in Section 10.

### 4.3 Nature and Purpose
Processing includes receiving, analyzing, transforming, storing limited logs/metadata, and returning governed outputs.

### 4.4 Categories of Data Subjects
May include Customer employees, contractors, users, and end customers, depending on Customer use case.

### 4.5 Categories of Personal Data
May include identifiers, communication content, account metadata, and other personal data submitted by Customer systems.

## 5. Processor Obligations
Processor shall:
1. Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer, unless required by law.
2. Ensure authorized personnel are bound by confidentiality obligations.
3. Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures.
4. Assist Customer with data subject requests and compliance obligations as reasonably required.
5. Maintain records of processing activities where required.
6. Notify Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of a Security Incident.

## 6. Security Measures
Processor maintains a security program including:
- logical access controls and role segregation,
- encryption in transit and at rest,
- vulnerability management and patching,
- audit logging and monitoring,
- incident response and business continuity controls.

Processor may update security controls provided changes do not materially diminish security of the Services.

## 7. Subprocessors
1. Customer grants general authorization for use of Subprocessors to provide the Services.
2. Processor will impose data protection obligations on Subprocessors substantially equivalent to this DPA.
3. Processor remains responsible for Subprocessor performance of applicable obligations.
4. Processor will provide notice mechanism for Subprocessor changes where commercially practicable.

## 8. International Data Transfers
Where Personal Data is transferred across borders, parties will implement a valid transfer mechanism under Applicable Data Protection Law (e.g., SCCs or equivalent safeguards where required).

## 9. Data Subject Rights and Assistance
Taking into account processing nature, Processor will provide reasonable assistance for:
- access, correction, deletion, portability, objection, and restriction requests,
- DPIA support where required,
- regulatory consultation support where legally required and commercially reasonable.

## 10. Return and Deletion
Upon termination/expiration of Services and subject to legal obligations, Processor will delete or return Personal Data per Customer instructions within agreed deletion windows. Backup lifecycle and legal hold exceptions may apply.

## 11. Security Incident Notification
Processor will:
1. Notify Customer without undue delay after confirming a Security Incident affecting Personal Data.
2. Provide available details: nature, likely consequences, and mitigation steps.
3. Take reasonable measures to contain, investigate, and remediate.
4. Provide updates as material information becomes available.

## 12. Audit and Information Rights
Processor will make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and may satisfy requests through third-party assessments, certifications (if any), security documentation, and limited audits under confidentiality and operational safeguards.

## 13. Confidentiality
Each party will treat all confidential information received under this DPA as confidential and use it only for performance of obligations under the Services Agreement and this DPA.

## 14. Liability
Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability framework set out in the underlying Services Agreement, except where prohibited by law.

## 15. Order of Precedence
In case of conflict between this DPA and the Services Agreement regarding data protection, this DPA controls for data protection matters.

## 16. Governing Law
As set forth in the underlying Services Agreement unless required otherwise by Applicable Data Protection Law.
